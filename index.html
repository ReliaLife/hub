RefineryRBD

A browser-based Monte Carlo reliability and financial simulator for refineries.

RefineryRBD models a refinery as a graph of process units, inventory buffers, and boundary sources and sinks, then runs a Monte Carlo simulation that translates reliability into the operational and financial language a planner can act on. Everything runs in your browser through a Web Worker: no backend, and no data leaves your machine. Cases persist locally and export to Excel or JSON.

Sweep availability and MTTR scenarios to see the mean EDC you can expect, find which units contribute most to cascade losses, and measure how much protection your inventory actually delivers. Two optimization layers go further: inventory capacity optimization sweeps each tank to find its financial optimum, and operating-level optimization finds the starting set points that maximize system EDC. Both detect when the response is flat and say so, rather than reporting noise as a recommendation.

Who it's for

Refinery engineers, reliability planners, and investment analysts who need defensible answers to questions like: what mean EDC can we expect at 97% availability with a 3-day MTTR? Which units drive the biggest cascade losses? How much inventory does a tank actually need to deliver the protection it claims? What is the dollar-per-year value of perfecting H2 redundancy?

Weatherstation Höf

A passion project: a homemade Arduino weather station, live from my house.

Weatherstation Höf is a do-it-yourself Arduino weather station running at home, streaming its readings to a small live web dashboard. A lightweight serverless backend caches the latest measurements so the page stays quick to load.

Why it's here

Mostly for the fun of building it. It's a placeholder on this page while ReliaLife's core tools are in development, and it will likely drop off the list as those tools come online.
